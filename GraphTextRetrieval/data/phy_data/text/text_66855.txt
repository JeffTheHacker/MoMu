N(4)-acetylsulfamethazine is a sulfonamide that is benzenesulfonamide substituted by an acetylamino group at position 4 and a 4,6-dimethyl-pyrimidin-2-yl group at the nitrogen atom. It is a metabolite of the antibiotic sulfamethazine. It has a role as a marine xenobiotic metabolite. It is a sulfonamide, a member of acetamides and a member of pyrimidines.
