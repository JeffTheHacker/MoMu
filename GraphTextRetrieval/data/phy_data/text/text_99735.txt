4-hydroxycyclophosphamide is a phosphorodiamide that consists of 2-amino-1,3,2-oxazaphosphinan-4-ol 2-oxide having two 2-chloroethyl groups attached to the exocyclic nitrogen. It has a role as an alkylating agent and a metabolite. It is a nitrogen mustard, an organochlorine compound and a phosphorodiamide.
