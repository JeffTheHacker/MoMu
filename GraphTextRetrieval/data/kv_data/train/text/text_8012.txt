C. i. natural red 20, also known as it or alkanna red, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as naphthoquinones. Naphthoquinones are compounds containing a naphthohydroquinone moiety, which consists of a benzene ring linearly fused to a bezene-1, 4-dione (quinone). C. i. natural red 20 exists as a solid and is considered to be practically insoluble (in water) and relatively neutral. Within the cell, c. i. natural red 20 is primarily located in the cytoplasm.
