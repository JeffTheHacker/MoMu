2, 5-Dihydro-2, 4, 5-trimethylthiazole, also known as 2, 4, 5-trimethyl-3-thiazoline, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as thiazolines. These are heterocyclic compounds containing a five-member unsaturated aliphatic ring with one nitrogen atom, one sulfur atom, three carbon atoms. 2, 5-Dihydro-2, 4, 5-trimethylthiazole is slightly soluble (in water) and a very strong basic compound (based on its pKa). Within the cell, 2, 5-dihydro-2, 4, 5-trimethylthiazole is primarily located in the cytoplasm.
