4, 5-Dihydro-2-methylthiazole, also known as it or 2-methyl-4, 5-dihydro-1, 3-thiazole, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as thiazolines. These are heterocyclic compounds containing a five-member unsaturated aliphatic ring with one nitrogen atom, one sulfur atom, three carbon atoms. 4, 5-Dihydro-2-methylthiazole exists as a solid, slightly soluble (in water), and a very strong basic compound (based on its pKa). Within the cell, 4, 5-dihydro-2-methylthiazole is primarily located in the cytoplasm. 4, 5-Dihydro-2-methylthiazole has a meaty, musty, and nutty taste.
