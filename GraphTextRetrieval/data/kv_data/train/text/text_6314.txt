it is a member of the class of (trifluoromethyl)benzenes that is 2,3-difluoro-6-(trifluoromethyl)benzenecarboxamidine in which the hydrogen attached to the imino nitrogen is replaced by a cyclopropylmethoxy group while one of the hydrogens attached to the other nitrogen is replaced by a phenylacetyl group. It is used as a fungicide for the control of powdery mildew in cereal crops as well as in apple and pear orchards. It has a role as an antifungal agrochemical. It is a carboxamidine, an oxime O-ether, a member of (trifluoromethyl)benzenes, a difluorobenzene and an amide fungicide.
