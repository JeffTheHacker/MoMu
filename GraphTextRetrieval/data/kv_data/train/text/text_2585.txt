it is a 7 amino acid peptide used to treat hypoactive sexual desire disorder in premenopausal women. it does not interact with alcohol. The mechanism by which it's action on receptors translates to a clinical effect is still unknown.  it was first described in the literature in 2003 when it was known by the investigational code it. Since then it was investigated for its place in treating sexual dysfunction in men and women but is now only indicated for women. Other drugs used to treat female sexual dysfunction include [flibanserin], [estrogen], [ospemifene], and [prasterone].  it was granted FDA approval on 21 June 2019.
