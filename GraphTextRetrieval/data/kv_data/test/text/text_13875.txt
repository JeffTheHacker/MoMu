Tetraethyl dithiopyrophosphate is an organic phosphate pesticide which acts as an inhibitor of cholinesterase, and as such it is highly toxic by all routes of exposure. It is a colorless to pale yellow mobile oil with an odor of garlic. It may be found as a liquid or as a dry mixture where the liquid is absorbed onto a dry carrier. it is an organic thiophosphate and an organothiophosphate insecticide. It has a role as an EC 3.1.1.7 (acetylcholinesterase) inhibitor, an EC 3.1.1.8 (cholinesterase) inhibitor, an acaricide and an agrochemical. It derives from a dithiodiphosphoric acid.
