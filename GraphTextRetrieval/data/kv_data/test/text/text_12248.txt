It is a N-iminopiperazine, a N-methylpiperazine, a hydrazone, a cyclic ketal, a semisynthetic derivative and a member of rifamycins. It is a tautomer of a rifampicin zwitterion. Rifampin is a semisynthetic derivative of rifamycin with broad antibacterial activity. Rifampin inhibits DNA-dependent RNA polymerase in susceptible bacteria and is often used in combination with other antibiotics for various infections including tuberculosis.
