4-Methyl-2-phenyl-1, 3-dioxolane, also known as it or fema 2130, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as benzene and substituted derivatives. These are aromatic compounds containing one monocyclic ring system consisting of benzene. 4-Methyl-2-phenyl-1, 3-dioxolane is slightly soluble (in water) and an extremely weak basic (essentially neutral) compound (based on its pKa). Within the cell, 4-methyl-2-phenyl-1, 3-dioxolane is primarily located in the cytoplasm. 4-Methyl-2-phenyl-1, 3-dioxolane is a sweet, bitter, and naphthalic tasting compound that can be found in alcoholic beverages. This makes 4-methyl-2-phenyl-1, 3-dioxolane a potential biomarker for the consumption of this food product.
