2,8-dioxoadenine is an oxopurine that is adenine bearing two oxo substituents at positions 2 and 8. It has a role as a nephrotoxic agent, a mammalian metabolite and a human urinary metabolite. It is an oxopurine and a member of 6-aminopurines. It derives from an adenine. It is a tautomer of a 2,8-dihydroxyadenine.
