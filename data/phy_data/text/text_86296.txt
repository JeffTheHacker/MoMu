Mepanipyrim is a member of the class of aminopyrimidines that is N-phenylpyrimidin-2-amine carrying additional methyl and 1-propynyl substituents at positions 4 and 6 respectively. A fungicide used to control a wide range of diseases including grey mould on strawberries, tomatoes and cucumabers, and scab on apples and pears. It has a role as an aryl hydrocarbon receptor agonist, a hepatotoxic agent and an antifungal agrochemical. It is an aminopyrimidine, a secondary amino compound, an acetylenic compound and an anilinopyrimidine fungicide.
