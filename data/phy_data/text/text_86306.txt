Triazamate is a triazole insecticide that is 1H-1,2,4-triazole which is substituted at positions 1, 3, and 5 by N,N-dimethylaminocarbonyl, tert-butyl, and (2-ethoxy-2-oxoethyl)sulfanediyl groups, respectively. It has a role as an EC 3.1.1.7 (acetylcholinesterase) inhibitor. It is a triazole insecticide, an aryl sulfide and an ethyl ester. It derives from a hydride of a 1H-1,2,4-triazole.
