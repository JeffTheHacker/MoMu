Silafluofen is an organosilicon compound that is dimethylsilane in which one of the hydrogens attached to the silicon is replaced by a 4-ethoxyphenyl group, while the other is replaced by a 3-(4-fluoro-3-phenoxyphenyl)propyl group. It is an insecticide used to control soil-borne insects. It has a role as a pyrethroid ether insecticide. It is an organofluorine compound, an organosilicon compound and an aromatic ether. It derives from a 4-(trimethylsilyl)phenol.
