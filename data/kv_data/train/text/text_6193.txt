2', 4'-Dimethylacetophenone, also known as (2, 4-dimethylphenyl)ethanone or it, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as alkyl-phenylketones. These are aromatic compounds containing a ketone substituted by one alkyl group, and a phenyl group. 2', 4'-Dimethylacetophenone is considered to be a practically insoluble (in water) and relatively neutral molecule. Within the cell, 2', 4'-dimethylacetophenone is primarily located in the cytoplasm. 2', 4'-Dimethylacetophenone is a sweet, floral, and mimosa tasting compound that can be found in tea. This makes 2', 4'-dimethylacetophenone a potential biomarker for the consumption of this food product.
