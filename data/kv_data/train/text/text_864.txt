N-aminopropylmorpholine appears as a colorless liquid with a faint, fishlike odor. Burns, but requires some effort to ignite. Corrosive to tissue. Produces toxic oxides of nitrogen during combustion. it is a member of the class of morpholines that is morpholine substituted by a 3-aminopropyl group a the N atom. It is a member of morpholines and a primary amino compound. It derives from a morpholine.
