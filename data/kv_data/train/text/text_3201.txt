5-Acetyl-2, 3-dihydro-1H-pyrrolizine belongs to the class of organic compounds known as pyrrolizines. Pyrrolizines are compounds containing a pyrrolizine moiety, which consists of a pyrrole ring fused to a pyrrolidine ring. 5-Acetyl-2, 3-dihydro-1H-pyrrolizine exists as a solid, slightly soluble (in water), and an extremely weak acidic (essentially neutral) compound (based on its pKa). Within the cell, 5-acetyl-2, 3-dihydro-1H-pyrrolizine is primarily located in the cytoplasm. 5-Acetyl-2, 3-dihydro-1H-pyrrolizine is a medicine tasting compound that can be found in alcoholic beverages, cereals and cereal products, and green vegetables. This makes 5-acetyl-2, 3-dihydro-1H-pyrrolizine a potential biomarker for the consumption of these food products.
