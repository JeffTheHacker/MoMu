4, 5-Dimethylthiazole, also known as fema 3274, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as 4, 5-disubstituted thiazoles. 4, 5-disubstituted thiazoles are compounds containing a thiazole ring substituted at positions 4 and 5 only. 4, 5-Dimethylthiazole exists as a solid, slightly soluble (in water), and a strong basic compound (based on its pKa). Within the cell, 4, 5-dimethylthiazole is primarily located in the cytoplasm. 4, 5-Dimethylthiazole has a boiled shrimp, fishy, and green taste.
