2, 6-Dimethoxy-4-methylphenol, also known as 2, 6-dimethoxy-p-cresol or 4-methyl-2, 6-dimethoxyphenol, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as methoxyphenols. Methoxyphenols are compounds containing a methoxy group attached to the benzene ring of a phenol moiety. 2, 6-Dimethoxy-4-methylphenol exists as a solid, slightly soluble (in water), and a very weakly acidic compound (based on its pKa). Within the cell, 2, 6-dimethoxy-4-methylphenol is primarily located in the cytoplasm. 2, 6-Dimethoxy-4-methylphenol is a caramel, eugenol, and medicinal tasting compound that can be found in fishes. This makes 2, 6-dimethoxy-4-methylphenol a potential biomarker for the consumption of this food product.
