Erioglaucine a, also known as brilliant blue 9 or c. i. acid blue 9, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as phenylbenzamines. These are aromatic compounds consisting of a benzyl group that is N-linked to a benzamine. Erioglaucine a is considered to be a practically insoluble (in water) and relatively neutral molecule. Within the cell, erioglaucine a is primarily located in the cytoplasm.
