Di-alpha-it, also known as 2, 2'-it or bis(2-furylmethyl) ether, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as heteroaromatic compounds. Heteroaromatic compounds are compounds containing an aromatic ring where a carbon atom is linked to an hetero atom. Di-alpha-it is considered to be a practically insoluble (in water) and relatively neutral molecule. Within the cell, di-alpha-it is primarily located in the cytoplasm. Di-alpha-it has a coffee and nutty taste.
