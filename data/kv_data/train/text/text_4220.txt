2, 5-Dihydro-2, 4-dimethyloxazole, also known as 2, 4-dimethyl-3-oxazoline, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as oxazolines. These are organic compounds containing 1, 3-oxazoline, a five-membered ring with a nitrogen and an oxygen atoms at the 1- and 3-position, respectively. Additionally, it contains two double bonds. 2, 5-Dihydro-2, 4-dimethyloxazole is soluble (in water) and a moderately basic compound (based on its pKa). Within the cell, 2, 5-dihydro-2, 4-dimethyloxazole is primarily located in the cytoplasm. Outside of the human body, 2, 5-dihydro-2, 4-dimethyloxazole can be found in nuts. This makes 2, 5-dihydro-2, 4-dimethyloxazole a potential biomarker for the consumption of this food product.
