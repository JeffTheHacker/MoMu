Tyrosine and derivatives are compounds containing tyrosine or a derivative thereof resulting from reaction of tyrosine at the amino group or the carboxy group, or from the replacement of any hydrogen of glycine by a heteroatom. it is slightly soluble (in water) and a moderately acidic compound (based on its pKa). it has been detected in multiple biofluids, such as urine, blood, and cerebrospinal fluid. it has been found to be associated with several diseases known as epilepsy, early-onset, vitamin b6-dependent and pyridoxamine 5-prime-phosphate oxidase deficiency; it has also been linked to the inborn metabolic disorders including aromatic l-amino acid decarboxylase deficiency.
