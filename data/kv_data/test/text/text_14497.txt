Alanyl-glutamic acid, also known as it or it, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as dipeptides. These are organic compounds containing a sequence of exactly two alpha-amino acids joined by a peptide bond. Alanyl-glutamic acid is slightly soluble (in water) and a weakly acidic compound (based on its pKa). Alanyl-glutamic acid has been primarily detected in feces.
