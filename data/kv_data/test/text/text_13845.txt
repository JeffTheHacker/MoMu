it, also known as it or 5-methyl-2-carboxaldehyde-thiophene, belongs to the class of organic compounds known as 2, 5-disubstituted thiophenes. These are organic compounds containing a thiophene that is disubstituted at the C-2, and C5-positions. it is considered to be a practically insoluble (in water) and relatively neutral molecule. Within the cell, it is primarily located in the cytoplasm. it exists in all eukaryotes, ranging from yeast to humans. it has a sweet, acetophenone, and almond taste.
